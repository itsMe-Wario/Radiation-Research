Manual Input

Glucose

A key blood sugar level indicator, important for diagnosing and monitoring diabetes.

Cholesterol

A fat-like substance vital for building cells and hormones. Comes in different forms like LDL and HDL.

Hemoglobin

Protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen. Its levels reflect oxygen transport capacity.

White Blood Cells

Defend the body against infections. Total count reflects immune system activity.

Red Blood Cells

Carry oxygen from the lungs to the body. Count indicates overall oxygen delivery.

Hematocrit

Proportion of red blood cells in blood. Indicates blood viscosity and oxygen-carrying capacity.

Insulin

Hormone that regulates blood sugar by helping cells absorb glucose. Measured to evaluate glucose metabolism.

BMI

Calculates body fat based on height and weight. Commonly used to classify weight status.

Systolic BP

Pressure when the heart beats. The top number in a blood pressure reading.

Diastolic BP

Pressure when the heart rests between beats. The bottom number in a BP reading.

Triglycerides

Type of fat stored in blood. High levels can affect heart health and metabolism.

HbA1c

Reflects average blood glucose over past 2-3 months. Key marker for glucose control.

LDL Cholesterol

Often called "bad cholesterol". High levels can lead to plaque buildup in arteries.

HDL Cholesterol

Known as "good cholesterol". Helps remove LDL from the bloodstream.

ALT

Enzyme found in liver cells. Levels reflect liver cell activity and health.

AST

Another enzyme in liver, heart, and muscles. Often measured with ALT for liver function.

Heart Rate

Number of heartbeats per minute. Indicates cardiovascular fitness and stress.

Creatinine

Waste product from muscle metabolism. Filtered by kidneys and used to assess kidney function.

Troponin

Protein found in heart muscles. Levels rise after heart muscle damage or strain.

C-reactive Protein

Produced by the liver in response to inflammation. Levels reflect general inflammation in the body.
